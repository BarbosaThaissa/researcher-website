Aeolian processes and landforms

OVERVIEW

Aeolian processes and landforms involve wind-driven activities, such as sand dune formation and desertification. Extensive aeolian fields are sedimentary records with significant potential to indicate paleoclimatic and paleoenvironmental changes throughout the Quaternary period. Analyzing the geomorphology of preserved aeolian landforms and their spatial and temporal distribution allows us to infer their origins and the dominant wind patterns that shaped them. Consequently, research on the expansion of sandy deserts makes the aeolian record a valuable proxy for reconstructing paleoenvironmental and paleoclimatic changes.

In South America, while sand seas and aeolian fields exhibit unique geoforms and show strong chronological correlations with other Southern Hemisphere deserts, the aeolian activity record remains relatively unexplored.

My primary research focus in this area has been on aeolian records (aeolian fields and loess) in Northeast Brazil. For instance, I have studied the aeolian field of the middle São Francisco, one of the most significant continental aeolian records in South America. I have also mapped different types of landforms and evaluated the factors and mechanisms governing the formation and expansion of the aeolian field along the middle São Francisco River plain. Loess deposits have also been described and dated in the Vaza-Barris River. Additionally, we have assessed recent reactivation processes of the field due to regional climate changes.

These studies aim to contribute to land conservation and climate change mitigation efforts.
