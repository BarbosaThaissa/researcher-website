About Me

Biography

Landerlei A. Santos holds a Ph.D. in Geosciences and Environment, with a focus on Geomorphology, Quaternary Research, Near-surface Geophysics, and Earth and Planetary Surface Processes. He is currently a Postdoctoral Fellow at the China University of Geosciences. Dr. Landerlei Santos obtained his Bachelor’s degree in Geology from the Federal University of Sergipe (UFS) in 2012 and a Master’s degree in Geosciences and Environment from São Paulo State University in 2015. His Ph.D. research at São Paulo State University was centered on the geomorphological mapping and evolution of the fluvial-aeolian system in the Middle São Francisco River region in Northeast Brazil. As part of the Latrubesse Research Group within the program “Large Rivers: Long Term Basins Evolution, Morphodynamics, and Global Change,” Landerlei served as a Research Assistant at Nanyang Technological University (Singapore) at the Asia School of the Environment-ASE and the Earth Observatory of Singapore. Throughout his academic career, Landerlei Santos has been involved in the MARSEAL Project (Petrobras-Fapese-UFS, 2008 to 2014), where he conducted research on Quaternary geology, focusing on the evolution of the continental margin of the Sergipe-Alagoas sedimentary basin during the Quaternary period. He has contributed to international geomorphology research projects, including those funded by the National Science Foundation (NSF-USA), the University of Texas, and the University of Florida. His research has involved the study of fluvial systems in the Brazilian and Bolivian Amazon (such as the Solimões, Negro, Amazonas, Tapajós, Beni, Mamoré, and Madeira rivers) and the Pampean wind systems in Argentina, a project supported by the National Geographic Society.

Research interests

Landerlei has research interest in the study of landforms and geomorphological processes across fluvial, aeolian, coastal, marine, antropogenic activities, and planetary environments. Currently I have directed my research efforts towards the São Francisco River region in Brazil, the Yangtze River in Asia, and the far side of the Moon, specifically the region targeted by the Chinese mission Chang’e-4.
