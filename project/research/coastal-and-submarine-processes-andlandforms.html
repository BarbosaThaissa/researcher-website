Coastal and Submarine Processes and Landforms

OVERVIEW

Coastal and submarine processes and landforms investigate shoreline dynamics and submerse landscapes, which is impotant for coastal protection and marine resource management. The coastal and marine areas, both shallow and deep, are influenced by complex interactions between continental and marine forces. Climate and human activities can shift the dynamic equilibrium, leading to new scenarios. Examples include changes in surface runoff, sea level, and natural resource use. River discharge changes due to damming can significantly impact river mouths, coastal areas, and adjacent continental shelves.

Environmental change assessments in coastal and submerged regions, heavily influenced by river discharges, must include river-ocean interaction evaluations. These studies are important for understanding the morpho-sedimentary context of continental shelves and assessing recent changes due to sediment supply alterations caused by human activities in river basins.

My research focus has been on the coastal and submarine areas of the Sergipe-Alagoas basin, Northeast Brazil, aiming to enhance the characterization of these environments and explain the interactions between the continent and the sea over the past centuries
