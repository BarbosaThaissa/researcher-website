Antropogenic Geomorphology

OVERVIEW

Anthropogenic geomorphology examines how human activities alter natural landforms, transforming them into anthropogenic landscapes. As these features change, mapping them requires a methodological approach that employs various tools to recognize landscapes reshaped by human interference

Currently, this is a key research focus on the Yangtze River, where I am working to enhance our understanding of the erosion and sedimentation balance in environments influenced by hydraulic engineering structures.

I have also contributed to technical research analyzing the impact of dams on the Amazon Basin and have studied and mapped the geomorphology of several urban rivers. These studies aim to guide sustainable land use and environmental planning.
