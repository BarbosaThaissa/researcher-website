OVERVIEW

River systems, particularly in large rivers (>1000 m³/s), typically build extensive floodplains that exhibit surfaces configured as complex associations of depositional forms. Various geomorphological styles are significant sedimentary records resulting from multiple stages of fluvial sedimentation, primarily governed by climatic changes, tectonic controls, and autogenic processes. Therefore, a better understanding of fluvial processes and landforms allows for the reconstruction of the fluvial landscape and can provide key information about paleoclimatic and paleoenvironmental conditions on continental areas throughout the Quaternary period. Additionally, understanding erosion and sediment transport processes helps in better water resource management and flood control, for instance.

This is a central focus of my interest, generally aiming to analyze, describe, and map floodplains. By combining sedimentological, geochronological, and shallow geophysical studies, I aim to improve our understanding of the processes and factors that led to the development of Quaternary floodplains. Regionally, studies have been focused on the São Francisco River (NE-Brazil) and other smaller rivers in NE Brazil. I have also collaborated with research in rivers of the Amazon Basin. Currently, I am studying the Yangtze River.
